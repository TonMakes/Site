Gum Care Guide

Recognizing the early signs of gum discomfort can help you take better daily care.

  • Redness or swelling may indicate irritation.
  • Bleeding after brushing can signal sensitivity.
  • Persistent bad breath may be linked to gum concerns.
  • Receding gum line should be monitored over time.

Oral Health Tips

Simple, everyday habits that may help keep your mouth fresh and your smile healthy.

  • Brush gently for 2 minutes with a soft-bristle brush, morning and night.
  • Clean between teeth daily using floss or interdental brushes.
  • Wait ~30 minutes after acidic drinks before brushing to help protect enamel.
  • Care for your tongue with a quick scrape or brush to help with freshness.
  • Stay hydrated; sugar-free gum may help maintain saliva flow.
  • Replace your brush every ~3 months or sooner if bristles fray.
